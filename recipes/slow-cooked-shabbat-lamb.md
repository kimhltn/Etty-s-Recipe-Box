# Slow Cooked Shabbat Lamb — Cook Mode

*Alison Roman's slow-cooked lamb, untouched — just restructured for the counter. Plan ahead twice: salt the lamb up to a day before, and the cook itself is 10–12 hands-off hours (overnight is the move — in the oven Thursday night, falling apart by Friday). The dressed-up sibling lives at `recipes/slow-cooked-shabbat-lamb-luxe.md`.*

**Serves:** 6–10 | **Prep:** 15 min (+ 12–24 hr salt ahead, ideally) | **Cook:** 10–12 hr (8 hr if boneless) | **Total:** overnight — lamb in the oven the night before you want it

---

## 🛒 Grocery List

### Meat & Dairy
- [ ] 1 bone-in lamb shoulder or leg, 4–5 lb (*or 3–4 lb boneless*)
- [ ] Greek yogurt or labne (~1½ cups, for serving)

### Produce
- [ ] 2 lb waxy potatoes (Yukon Gold or fingerlings)
- [ ] 2 heads garlic (going in whole and unpeeled — plus 1 clove for the yogurt)
- [ ] 2 lemons (1 for the pot, 1 for serving)
- [ ] 1 bunch oregano, marjoram, or thyme
- [ ] 1–2 cups tender herbs for serving — parsley, dill, chives, mint, any mix

### Pantry
- [ ] 1 bottle (750 ml) dry white wine — crisp and unfancy, pinot grigio territory
- [ ] Olive oil (a generous pour, ~½ cup)
- [ ] Kosher salt & black pepper

**Equipment check:** a Dutch oven or heavy pot of **at least 6 quarts, with a lid**. The whole recipe happens in it.

---

## Before You Start (5 minutes, day of)

*Assumes the lamb was salted yesterday — that's Step 1.*

- **Potatoes:** halve any bigger than an egg; leave small ones whole. No peeling.
- **Garlic:** halve both heads crosswise, skins on. Set 1 clove aside for the yogurt.
- **Lemon:** halve 1 lemon crosswise. Cut the other into wedges for the table (can wait until serving).
- **Yogurt sauce:** stir **~1½ cups yogurt or labne** with **1 grated garlic clove**, salt, and pepper. Refrigerate until serving.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Salt the Lamb (ideally 12–24 hours ahead)

1. Season the **lamb** generously all over with **salt and pepper** — it's a big piece of meat, so be bolder than feels polite. Refrigerate, uncovered if you can, **12–24 hours**. *Same-day works in a pinch; ahead is noticeably better on a cut this size.*

### Step 2 — Build the Pot (15 minutes, then you're done)

1. Preheat the oven to **275°F / 135°C**. Set the **lamb** in the Dutch oven.
2. Scatter the **potatoes**, **2 halved garlic heads**, **halved lemon**, and the **whole herb bunch** around (and under, where they fit) the lamb.
3. Pour the **entire bottle of wine** over everything, then add a **long pour of olive oil** — you want a visible, thick layer floating on top. It bastes the lamb all night.

### Step 3 — The Long Nothing (10–12 hours)

1. Lid on. Into the oven for **10–12 hours** (**~8 hours** if boneless) — and per Alison: *do not look, peek, or touch for at least 10 hours.* No basting, no checking.
2. It's done when the meat **shreds at the touch of a spoon and the bone wiggles free** — timing here is very flexible, and an extra hour won't hurt it.

### Step 4 — Serve

1. Remove from the oven and let it settle **~15 minutes** with the lid off. *(Now's the moment to pick/tear the tender herbs and cut lemon wedges if you haven't.)*
2. Pull the lamb apart in big pieces onto a platter — it will fall apart on its own. Spoon the **potatoes, garlic, and plenty of pot juices** around it.
3. Shower with the **tender herbs**, a drizzle of **olive oil**, and serve with the **garlicky yogurt** and **lemon wedges** alongside. Squeeze the soft confit garlic out of its skins as you eat — great on bread.

---

## Tips

- **Overnight math:** in the oven at 8–9 pm → done 7–9 am. It reheats beautifully, so cooking it the night before a dinner party is the low-stress play.
- **Make-ahead:** keeps covered in the fridge **3–4 days**. Reheat gently, covered, at **275°F** until warmed through.
- **Bread or rice** at the table is non-negotiable — the wine-lamb juices are half the dish.
- **Leftovers** shred into pasta, flatbreads with the leftover yogurt, or grain bowls.
