# Masoor Dal (Weeknight) — Cook Mode

**Serves:** 4–6 | **Prep:** 12 min (+30 min lentil soak, overlaps) | **Cook:** ~45 min | **Total:** ~55 min

---

## 🛒 Grocery List

### Produce
- [ ] 1 yellow onion (need 1 cup chopped)
- [ ] 1 medium sweet potato (~10 oz)
- [ ] Fresh ginger (need 1 tbsp minced)
- [ ] 1 head garlic (need 2 cloves minced + 3 thinly sliced)
- [ ] 1 Thai or bird's-eye red chili
- [ ] 1 bunch cilantro (need 3 tbsp chopped)
- [ ] 1 lime

### Pantry
- [ ] Coconut oil (2 tbsp) — *or vegetable oil*
- [ ] 1 cup red lentils
- [ ] 1 can chopped tomatoes (need 1 cup)
- [ ] 1 can full-fat coconut milk (need ½ can)
- [ ] Ghee or neutral oil (2 tbsp, for the tarka)
- [ ] Kosher salt

### Spices
- [ ] Ground coriander (2 tsp)
- [ ] Ground cumin (2 tsp)
- [ ] Turmeric (2 tsp)
- [ ] Ground ginger (1 tsp)
- [ ] Cumin seeds (1½ tsp, for the tarka)

---

## Before You Start (5-Minute Prep + a Soak)

- **Lentils — do this first:** rinse **1 cup red lentils** in a fine-mesh strainer until the water runs mostly clear, then cover with plenty of cold water and soak **~30 minutes** — easier on digestion, and they cook faster and creamier. Everything below happens while they soak. **Drain and discard the soak water** just before they go in the pot.
- **Garlic:** mince 2 cloves (pot); thinly slice 3 (tarka). Keep the piles separate.
- **Ginger:** mince 1 tbsp.
- **Chili:** finely dice (seeds in for more heat, out for mild).
- **Spices:** measure the 4 ground spices into one small bowl; cumin seeds stay separate for the tarka.
- **Coconut milk:** shake the can hard.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Build the Base

1. In a large saucepan over medium-low, heat **2 tbsp coconut oil** and sauté **1 cup chopped onion** until softened and translucent, **5–6 minutes**. *(While it cooks: peel and finely dice the sweet potato — you need 2½ cups.)*
2. Add the **sweet potato** and sauté **5 minutes**. Stir in **1 tbsp minced ginger** and **2 minced garlic cloves**, cook **1 minute**, and reduce heat to low.

### Step 2 — Bloom the Spices

1. Add the **diced chili** and the spice bowl — **2 tsp coriander, 2 tsp cumin, 2 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp ground ginger** — and stir into the oil **30–60 seconds**, until strongly fragrant. Don't let it scorch.
2. Add the **drained, soaked lentils** and stir until well coated.

### Step 3 — Simmer

1. Add **1 cup chopped tomatoes** and **3½ cups water** (reduced from the original 4 to make room for the coconut milk). Bring to a boil, then drop to a fast simmer.
2. Cook uncovered **about 20 minutes**, stirring occasionally — more often near the end, when the collapsing lentils start to stick — until the lentils have broken down and a piece of sweet potato mashes easily against the side of the pot. *(While it simmers: chop the cilantro, juice the half lime, set up the tarka skillet.)*

### Step 4 — Coconut Milk Finish

1. Season with salt (start with **1 tsp kosher**), then stir in **½ can coconut milk** (~¾ cup).
2. Simmer *gently* **10 minutes**, until thickened — don't hard-boil once the coconut milk is in, or it can split. Whisk briefly to amalgamate. Done when it's a loose porridge: a spoon dragged through leaves a trail that slowly closes.

### Step 5 — Tarka (last 5 minutes)

1. In a small skillet, heat **2 tbsp ghee or oil** over medium. Add **1½ tsp cumin seeds** — they should sputter within seconds.
2. Add the **3 sliced garlic cloves** and cook until just golden, **60–90 seconds**, then pull it off the heat immediately — golden to burnt is fast.

### Step 6 — Finish & Serve

1. Off heat, stir the **juice of ½ lime** into the dal. Taste for salt.
2. Bowl it, pour the sizzling tarka over the top, and finish with **chopped cilantro**.

## Tips

- **Freezing (this recipe's superpower):** freeze in flat portions; it thickens a lot when cold, so loosen with a splash of water while reheating. Best trick: make a **fresh 3-minute tarka over a reheated portion** — it tastes just-cooked.
- **The other half-can of coconut milk** freezes fine in a jar or ice-cube tray for the next batch.
- **In a real rush?** Skip the soak — texture is nearly identical, you just lose the digestion benefit. Simmer ~25 minutes instead of 20.
- **Heat:** one chili with seeds out is mild. Seeds in = medium; a second chili in the bloom = properly hot.
- **Vegan** as long as the tarka uses oil, not ghee.
- **Pairs with** rice or broiled salmon (Nigella's suggestion).
- **Feeling ambitious?** The dressed-up sibling lives at `recipes/masoor-dal-weekend.md` — browned onions, fried tomato base, the full tarka, and the charcoal-smoke flex.
