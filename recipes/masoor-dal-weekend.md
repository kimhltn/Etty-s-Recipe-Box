# Masoor Dal (Weekend) — Cook Mode

**Serves:** 4–6 | **Prep:** 12 min (+30 min lentil soak, overlaps) | **Cook:** ~60 min | **Total:** ~70 min

*The restaurant-level version: deeply browned onions, a tomato base fried until the ghee separates, and the full crowded tarka. For the fast version, see `recipes/masoor-dal-weeknight.md`.*

---

## 🛒 Grocery List

### Produce
- [ ] 1 yellow onion (need 1 cup chopped)
- [ ] 1 medium sweet potato (~10 oz)
- [ ] Fresh ginger (need 1 tbsp minced)
- [ ] 1 head garlic (need 2 cloves minced + 3 thinly sliced)
- [ ] 1 Thai or bird's-eye red chili
- [ ] 8–10 fresh curry leaves
- [ ] 1 bunch cilantro (need 3 tbsp chopped)
- [ ] 1 lime

### Meat & Dairy
- [ ] Ghee — 4 tbsp total (+1 tsp if doing the dhungar)
- [ ] Butter (1 tbsp, to finish)

### Pantry
- [ ] 1 cup red lentils
- [ ] 1 can chopped tomatoes (need 1 cup)
- [ ] 1 can full-fat coconut milk (need ½ can)
- [ ] Kosher salt
- [ ] Sugar (a small pinch, optional)

### Spices
- [ ] Ground coriander (2 tsp)
- [ ] Ground cumin (2 tsp)
- [ ] Turmeric (2 tsp)
- [ ] Ground ginger (1 tsp)
- [ ] Cumin seeds (1½ tsp)
- [ ] Black mustard seeds (1 tsp)
- [ ] 2 dried red chilies
- [ ] Hing / asafoetida (a pinch)
- [ ] Kasuri methi — dried fenugreek leaves (1 tbsp)

### Optional for serving
- [ ] 1 piece natural lump charcoal (for the dhungar smoke finish)

## Before You Start (5-Minute Prep + a Soak)

- **Lentils — do this first:** rinse **1 cup red lentils** until the water runs mostly clear, then cover with plenty of cold water and soak **~30 minutes** — easier on digestion, faster and creamier cooking. Everything below happens while they soak. **Drain and discard the soak water** before they go in the pot.
- **Garlic:** mince 2 cloves (pot); thinly slice 3 (tarka). Keep the piles separate.
- **Ginger:** mince 1 tbsp.
- **Chili:** finely dice the fresh chili (seeds in for more heat, out for mild).
- **Spices:** ground spices in one small bowl. Tarka crew in another: cumin seeds, mustard seeds, dried chilies, curry leaves, hing within reach — the tarka moves fast.
- **Kasuri methi:** measure 1 tbsp, ready to crush.
- **Coconut milk:** shake the can hard.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Bhuna the Onions (the flavor foundation)

1. In a large saucepan over medium, heat **2 tbsp ghee** and cook **1 cup chopped onion**, stirring more often as it darkens, until deep golden brown — **12–15 minutes**. A splash of water if it starts to catch; don't rush this, it's where most of the extra flavor lives. *(While it cooks: peel and finely dice the sweet potato — you need 2½ cups.)*
2. Add the **sweet potato** and sauté **5 minutes**. Stir in **1 tbsp minced ginger** and **2 minced garlic cloves**, cook **1 minute**, and reduce heat to medium-low.

### Step 2 — Bloom, Then Fry the Tomato Dry

1. Add the **diced chili** and the spice bowl — **2 tsp coriander, 2 tsp cumin, 2 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp ground ginger** — and stir into the fat **30–60 seconds**, until strongly fragrant. Don't let it scorch.
2. Add **1 cup chopped tomatoes** — no water yet — and fry, stirring, **5–6 minutes**, until the paste darkens, thickens, and the **ghee visibly separates at the edges**. That fat-separation moment is the classic doneness cue for a finished masala; wait for it.

### Step 3 — Simmer

1. Stir in the **drained, soaked lentils** to coat, then add **3½ cups water**. Bring to a boil, then drop to a fast simmer.
2. Cook uncovered **about 20 minutes**, stirring occasionally — more often near the end as the collapsing lentils start to stick — until the lentils have broken down and a piece of sweet potato mashes easily against the side of the pot. *(While it simmers: chop the cilantro, juice the half lime, stage the tarka skillet.)*

### Step 4 — Coconut Milk & Kasuri Methi Finish

1. Season with salt (start with **1 tsp kosher**), stir in **½ can coconut milk** (~¾ cup), and crush **1 tbsp kasuri methi** between your palms straight into the pot.
2. Simmer *gently* **10 minutes**, until thickened — don't hard-boil once the coconut milk is in, or it can split. Whisk briefly to amalgamate. Done when it's a loose porridge: a spoon dragged through leaves a trail that slowly closes.
3. Off heat: stir in **1 tbsp butter**, the **juice of ½ lime**, and a **small pinch of sugar** if the tomatoes taste sharp. Taste for salt.

### Step 5 — The Full Tarka (last 5 minutes)

1. In a small skillet, heat **2 tbsp ghee** over medium. Add **1½ tsp cumin seeds** and **1 tsp black mustard seeds** — within seconds they should sputter and pop.
2. Add **2 dried red chilies** and the **3 sliced garlic cloves**; cook until the garlic is just golden, **60–90 seconds**.
3. Off heat, immediately add **8–10 curry leaves** — they crackle hard, stand back — and a **pinch of hing**. Swirl once.

### Step 6 — Serve

1. Bowl the dal, pour the sizzling tarka over the top, and finish with **chopped cilantro**.
2. *Dinner-party flex — the dhungar:* nest a small steel bowl on top of the finished dal in the pot, drop in a piece of **lit lump charcoal**, spoon **1 tsp ghee** over it, and clamp the lid on **2–3 minutes**. Remove the bowl and serve immediately, smoky and dramatic.

## Tips

- **Freezing:** same superpower as the weeknight version — flat portions, loosen with water to reheat, and a **fresh tarka over a reheated portion** makes it taste just-cooked.
- **Curry leaves** freeze well in a zip bag straight from fresh; hing and kasuri methi keep near-forever. One Indian-grocery run stocks you for many batches.
- **The other half-can of coconut milk** freezes fine in a jar or ice-cube tray.
- **Heat:** the dried chilies in the tarka add aroma more than fire. For real heat, seeds in on the fresh chili, or add a second one to the bloom.
- **Vegan:** swap ghee for coconut oil throughout and skip the butter — you keep ~90% of the effect.
- **Pairs with** basmati rice, or broiled salmon.
