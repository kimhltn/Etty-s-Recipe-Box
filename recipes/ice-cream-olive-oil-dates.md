# Ice Cream with Olive Oil and Dates — Cook Mode

*The tiniest keeper in the box: a one-serving, 10-minute dessert that scales by plain multiplication — 1 date + 2 tsp oil per person.*

**Serves:** 1 (scales freely) | **Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 5 min | **Total:** 10 min

---

## 🛒 Grocery List

### Produce
- [ ] Medjool dates (1 per serving)

### Meat & Dairy
- [ ] Vanilla ice cream (1–2 scoops per serving) — *keep it in the freezer until the last second*

### Pantry
- [ ] Extra-virgin olive oil (2 tsp per serving) — *this is the flavor; use the good bottle*
- [ ] Flaky sea salt

---

## Before You Start (1-Minute Prep)

- **Date:** pit and tear into 3 or 4 pieces — the torn, ragged edges are what caramelize.
- **Ice cream:** stays in the freezer until the moment of assembly. Very cold ice cream against warm oil is the whole contrast.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Sizzle the Date

1. In a small skillet, heat **2 tsp extra-virgin olive oil** and the **torn date** over medium. Once it starts sizzling, give it **1–2 minutes** — the date should turn glossy and jammy at the edges and the oil smell fragrant — then turn off the heat. Don't push past the sizzle; dates scorch fast.

### Step 2 — Assemble Fast

1. Scoop **1–2 scoops very cold vanilla ice cream** into a bowl, then scrape the **dates and every drop of the oil** on top.
2. Sprinkle with a **pinch of flaky sea salt** and eat as soon as possible — it's built on the hot-oil-meets-cold-cream contrast.

---

## Tips

- **Scaling math:** 1 date + 2 tsp oil per serving; a small skillet comfortably handles 4 servings' worth in one go. Everything else is scooping.
- **Ice cream is flexible:** vanilla lets the toppings shine, but it's also great on coffee, hazelnut, or strawberry ice cream — or gelato.
- **The bitterness is the point:** a peppery, assertive olive oil plays the same role bitter chocolate sauce does against sweet ice cream. Mild oil works; bold oil sings.
- **No make-ahead, no leftovers** — this is a made-to-order dessert. Happily, the whole thing takes 10 minutes.
