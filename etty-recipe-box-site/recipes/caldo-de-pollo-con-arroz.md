# Caldo de Pollo con Arroz — Cook Mode

**Serves:** 6 | **Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 45 min | **Total:** ~60 min

## Before You Start (5-Minute Prep)

- **Onions:** halve both. Set aside one half whole (poach), slice one half into thick rounds (charring), and finely mince ¼ cup from the rest (rice).
- **Garlic:** smash and peel 2 cloves (poach); leave 6–8 cloves unpeeled (charring).
- **Celery:** finely mince ¼ cup (about 1 rib).
- **Jalapeño:** cut 4 thin slices, flick out the seeds.
- **Rice:** measure 1 cup basmati into a fine-mesh strainer, ready to rinse.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Poach the Chicken (start this first)

1. In a large pot, combine **8 cups low-sodium chicken broth**, **1½ lb bone-in skin-on chicken thighs** (about 4), **½ yellow onion** (left whole), **2 garlic cloves, smashed and peeled**, **2 bay leaves**, and **1½ tsp kosher salt**.
2. Bring to a bare simmer — don't boil, or the chicken toughens. Poach gently, partially covered, **20–25 minutes**, until the chicken hits 175°F and pulls apart easily. Skim foam as it rises.
3. Transfer the chicken to a plate. Fish out and discard the onion, garlic, and bay leaves; keep the broth warm. Once cool enough to handle, discard skin and bones and shred the meat. Don't add it back yet.
4. *Rotisserie shortcut: skip the poach — simmer the same aromatics in the broth 15 minutes, discard them, and add 1 lb shredded rotisserie meat in Step 4.*

### Step 2 — Spanish Basmati Rice (while the chicken poaches)

*This rice earned its own standalone recipe: `recipes/spanish-basmati-rice.md`.*

1. Rinse **1 cup basmati rice** under cold water until the water runs clear.
2. Heat **2 tbsp olive or avocado oil** in a saucepan over medium. Cook **¼ cup finely minced yellow onion** and **¼ cup finely minced celery** for 2 minutes, then stir in the rice and toast **2–3 minutes**, until lightly golden and fragrant.
3. Stir in **¼ cup tomato sauce**, **1½ tsp onion powder**, **1 tsp garlic powder**, **2 tsp dried parsley**, and **½ tsp kosher salt**.
4. Add **1½ cups low-sodium chicken broth**, bring to a boil, then cover and simmer on low **15 minutes**, until the liquid is absorbed.
5. Off heat, steam with the lid on **10 minutes**, then fluff with a fork.

### Step 3 — Char and Fry the Tomato Base (while everything simmers)

1. Set **3 whole Roma tomatoes**, **½ yellow onion in thick slices**, and **6–8 unpeeled garlic cloves** in a dry skillet over high heat (or under the broiler). Turn occasionally until blackened in spots and softened, **8–10 minutes**. Peel the garlic.
2. Blend the charred vegetables with **4 thin jalapeño slices (seeded)** and a splash of the poaching broth until smooth.
3. Wipe out the skillet, heat **2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil** over medium-high, and pour in the purée — it should sizzle hard. Fry, stirring, **4–5 minutes** until it darkens and thickens. Stir in **1 tsp ground cumin** and **1 tsp Mexican oregano** for the last 30 seconds.

### Step 4 — Bring It Together

1. Stir the fried tomato base into the pot of warm broth. Simmer, uncovered, **10 minutes**.
2. Add the shredded chicken and heat through, about **5 minutes** — no longer, so it stays juicy.
3. Off heat, stir in the **juice of 1 lime**. Taste and adjust salt — it likely needs another pinch or two.

### Step 5 — Serve

1. Spoon **¼ cup Spanish basmati rice** into each bowl and ladle the soup over.
2. Top each bowl with **sliced avocado**, **a dollop of sour cream**, **sliced green onions**, **chopped cilantro**, and **a few drops of habanero hot sauce**.
3. Serve with **lime wedges** and warm tortillas, Jack cheese, or tortilla chips.

---

## Tips

- **Store rice and soup separately** — basmati soaks up broth fast and goes soft if stored together.
- **Reheating:** warm the soup, then add rice for the last minute or two.
- **More heat?** Blend in extra jalapeño (or leave the seeds in), or char a serrano alongside the tomatoes.
- **Deeper broth:** if you have time, drop the stripped thigh bones back into the pot for the Step 4 simmer, then remove.
- **Salt in stages:** the poach, the rice, and the final taste-and-adjust each get their own — that's what makes it taste seasoned, not salty.
