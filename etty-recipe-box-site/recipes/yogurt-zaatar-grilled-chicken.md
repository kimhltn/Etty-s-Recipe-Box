# Yogurt Za'atar Grilled Chicken — Cook Mode

**Serves:** 4–6 | **Active:** ~30 min | **Marinade:** 2+ hrs (up to overnight) | **Day-of total:** ~30 min

*Plan-ahead note: mix the marinade at breakfast or lunch, and dinner is a 30-minute job.*

---

## 🛒 Grocery List

### Produce
- [ ] 1 head garlic (need 6 cloves)
- [ ] 2 lemons
- [ ] 1 bunch cilantro (¼ cup chopped + sprigs for garnish)
- [ ] Fresh oregano or marjoram (1 tbsp chopped + sprigs for garnish)

### Meat & Dairy
- [ ] 2¼ lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs
- [ ] 1 cup plain whole-milk yogurt

### Pantry
- [ ] Extra-virgin olive oil (3 tbsp + more for serving)
- [ ] Kosher salt
- [ ] Black pepper

### Spices
- [ ] Za'atar (1½ tbsp + more for serving)

### Optional for serving
- [ ] Pita or other flatbread
- [ ] Cucumbers + tomatoes (for a big salad alongside)

---

## Before You Start (5-Minute Prep)

This recipe is all about **splits** — almost every ingredient divides between the marinade and the sauce. Sort them now and the rest is easy:

- **Garlic:** grate or press all 6 cloves. **5 for the marinade, 1 set aside for the sauce.**
- **Lemons:** zest both. **Half the zest to the marinade, half set aside for the sauce.** Keep one of the zested lemons for serving (the spare goes back in the fridge for another day).
- **Yogurt:** measure 1 cup. **⅓ cup to the marinade, ⅔ cup set aside for the sauce.**
- **Herbs:** chop ¼ cup cilantro and 1 tbsp oregano or marjoram; save a few whole sprigs of each for garnish.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Marinate (morning of — needs 2+ hours)

1. In a large bowl, stir together **5 grated garlic cloves**, **half the lemon zest**, **⅓ cup yogurt**, **¼ cup chopped cilantro**, **3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil**, **1½ tbsp za'atar**, **1 tbsp chopped oregano or marjoram**, **1¾ tsp kosher salt**, and **¼ tsp black pepper**.
2. Add **2¼ lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs** and toss until every piece is coated. Cover and refrigerate **at least 2 hours**, up to overnight — longer sits deepen the flavor and tenderness, but even the 2-hour minimum delivers.

### Step 2 — Grill

1. Light the grill to **medium**. Pull the chicken from the bowl, shaking off the excess marinade.
2. Grill the first side until charred in spots, **5–8 minutes**. Flip and grill **another 5–8 minutes**, until just cooked through — **165°F** at the thickest point, juices running clear.
3. *Broiler alternate (rainy day): heat the broiler with the rack 3 inches from the element, and broil on a foil-lined sheet pan — foil, not parchment, which can burn — same times, same flip.*

### Step 3 — Yogurt Sauce (while the chicken grills)

1. Stir together the **reserved ⅔ cup yogurt**, **reserved grated garlic clove**, and **reserved lemon zest**. Season to taste with **salt and pepper**.
2. Cut the zested lemon in half, ready for serving.

### Step 4 — Serve

1. Pile the chicken on a platter. Drizzle with **olive oil** and add a **big squeeze of the zested lemon**.
2. Scatter over the **cilantro and oregano/marjoram sprigs** and a **pinch more za'atar**. Serve with the **yogurt sauce**, warm **pita**, and a **big cucumber-tomato salad**.

---

## Tips

- **Chicken breasts work too** — watch them closely; they cook faster than thighs and dry out if they wait on the slow pieces.
- **The marinade window is forgiving:** 2 hours minimum, overnight maximum. The yogurt tenderizes without turning the meat mushy the way citrus-heavy marinades can.
- **Don't skip shaking off the marinade** — too much clinging yogurt steams and scorches before the meat chars.
- **Doneness cue:** char in spots is flavor, not a mistake. Pull at 165°F; thighs stay juicy where breasts would punish you.
