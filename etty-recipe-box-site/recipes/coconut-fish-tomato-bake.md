# Coconut Fish and Tomato Bake (Etty's Version) — Cook Mode

**Serves:** 4 | **Active:** ~25 min | **Marinade:** 15–30 min (overlaps the prep) | **Total:** ~40 min

*The sequencing trick: the tomatoes' 10-minute head start happens during the back half of the fish's marinade — nothing waits on anything.*

---

## 🛒 Grocery List

### Produce
- [ ] 2-inch piece fresh ginger
- [ ] 2 garlic cloves
- [ ] 2 limes
- [ ] 1 bunch cilantro (½ cup chopped, split in two)
- [ ] 2 pints cherry or grape tomatoes

### Seafood
- [ ] 4 (6-oz) fillets tilapia — *or any white fish (snapper, haddock, fluke); salmon works too*

### Pantry
- [ ] 1 cup unsweetened coconut milk (1 can, most of it)
- [ ] Honey (1½ tsp)
- [ ] Olive oil (3 tbsp)
- [ ] Kosher salt

### Spices
- [ ] Ground turmeric (½ tsp)
- [ ] Red-pepper flakes (½ tsp)

### For serving
- [ ] Steamed rice *or* a baguette to grill and sop with

---

## Before You Start (5-Minute Prep)

- **Ginger + garlic:** finely grate the **2-inch piece of ginger** and **both garlic cloves**.
- **Cilantro:** chop ½ cup total — **¼ cup for the marinade, ¼ cup for serving**.
- **Tomatoes:** halve them all. *(This is the point — halved tomatoes go jammy and give up their juices to the sauce instead of bursting whole.)*
- **Racks:** one in the lower-middle, one closest to the broiler.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Marinade (start this first)

1. In a large bowl, whisk together **1 cup unsweetened coconut milk**, the **grated ginger and garlic**, **½ tsp ground turmeric**, **½ tsp red-pepper flakes**, **1½ tsp honey**, and **1 tsp kosher salt**.
2. **Zest and juice 1 lime** directly into the bowl. Stir in **¼ cup chopped cilantro**.
3. Add the **4 fish fillets** and turn to coat. Into the fridge for **15–30 minutes** — no longer, or the lime starts "cooking" the fish.

### Step 2 — Tomatoes On (while the fish marinates)

1. Heat the oven to **425°F**.
2. Pile the **halved tomatoes** onto a large sheet pan, drizzle with **2 tbsp olive oil**, season with **salt**, and toss.
3. About **10 minutes before the marinade is up**, slide the pan onto the lower-middle rack. This head start gets them collapsing and concentrating before the fish ever shows up.

### Step 3 — Fish Joins the Party

1. Pull the pan out. Nestle the **marinated fillets** between the tomatoes and spoon **all the marinade** from the bowl over the fish. Drizzle the fish with the last **1 tbsp olive oil**.
2. Back onto the lower-middle rack. Roast **8–10 minutes** (thickness-dependent), until the surface is opaque but the center isn't cooked through — it should **not** flake easily yet.
3. Remove the pan and heat the **broiler to high**.

### Step 4 — Broil to Finish

1. Move the pan up to the broiler rack. Broil **5–6 minutes**, rotating the pan once, until the fish is tender and flakes, and the tomatoes are browning in spots.
2. Meanwhile, slice the **second lime into wedges**.

### Step 5 — Serve

1. Divide the fish and tomatoes among plates and **tip the pan juices over the fish** — that silky turmeric-coconut sauce is the whole point.
2. Shower with the **remaining ¼ cup cilantro** and serve with **lime wedges**, over **steamed rice** or with **grilled baguette** for sopping.

---

## Tips

- **The sauce scales with the coconut milk** — the full 1 cup (vs. the original ¾) is what makes it generous enough for rice. Don't skimp.
- **Fish is flexible:** tilapia is the house default; anything from snapper to salmon works. Thicker fillets push both cook times to the top of their ranges.
- **Doneness insurance:** the two-stage cook (roast then broil) is forgiving, but pull the roast early if in doubt — the broiler finishes the job.
- **Don't marinate past 30 minutes** — lime juice is working on the fish the whole time.
