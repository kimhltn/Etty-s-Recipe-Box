# Spicy Vinegar Chicken over Tomatoes — Cook Mode

**Serves:** 4 | **Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** ~35 min | **Total:** ~45 min

*House cut: boneless, skin-on thighs — the skin builds the sauce, no bones means a short braise and easy eating. Any butcher counter will debone thighs on request (or DIY: one knife-run along each side of the bone, a scrape, done — freeze the bones for stock).*

---

## 🛒 Grocery List

### Produce
- [ ] 1 lb ripe tomatoes — *this is half the dish; worth the good ones*
- [ ] 1 red or yellow onion
- [ ] 1 head garlic (need 4 cloves)

### Meat & Dairy
- [ ] 2¼–2½ lb boneless, skin-on chicken thighs (about 8) — *ask the butcher to debone bone-in thighs, skin on*
- [ ] Butter (1 tbsp, optional finish)

### Pantry
- [ ] Olive oil (2 tbsp + drizzles)
- [ ] White distilled vinegar (½ cup)
- [ ] Chicken stock (⅓ cup — or water; stock adds back a little of what the bones would have)
- [ ] Kosher salt + flaky salt
- [ ] Black pepper
- [ ] Red pepper flakes (1½ tsp + more for serving)

### Optional for serving
- [ ] Crusty bread — *the sauce demands mopping*

---

## Before You Start (5-Minute Prep)

- **Chicken:** pat the thighs *very* dry — dry skin is what browns — and season both sides with salt and pepper.
- **Onion:** thinly slice the whole thing; set **¼ aside raw** for the platter, ¾ for the pot.
- **Garlic:** crush 4 cloves.
- **Ventilation:** crack a window or run the hood — simmering vinegar is punchy for a few minutes.

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Sear Hard (in batches)

1. Heat **2 tbsp olive oil** in a large heavy pot or deep skillet over medium-high. Add **half the thighs skin-side down** and set a **weight on top** (a foil-wrapped heavy pan, a bacon press, a lid that fits inside) for the **entire browning time** — full, even contact is what gets uniformly crispy skin. Leave them alone until the skin is deeply golden and releases without a fight, **6–8 minutes**. Flip, remove the weight, and brown **2–3 minutes**. Transfer to a plate; repeat with the rest, lowering the heat a touch if the fond darkens past deep brown.
2. Don't rush or crowd this step — with no bones, the fond and rendered fat are doing all the sauce-building. *(While the chicken sears: thinly slice the tomatoes.)*
3. *Cooking for a crowd? Sequential batches get slow — **brown in two pans at once** (proven 2026-07-23), deglaze the second pan with a **splash of white wine** to capture its fond, then combine everything into one pot for Steps 2–3 and build the sauce there.*

### Step 2 — Aromatics in the Fat

1. Reduce heat to medium. Add the **4 crushed garlic cloves** and **1½ tsp red pepper flakes** to the fat and sizzle **30–60 seconds**, until fragrant — don't let the garlic brown.
2. Scatter in the **¾ sliced onion** and cook, stirring and scraping up the fond, **2–3 minutes**, until slightly softened.

### Step 3 — Quick Braise

1. Return the chicken **skin-side up**, nestling the pieces together — keep the skin above the liquid line so it holds some crispness. Pour **½ cup white vinegar** and **⅓ cup chicken stock (or water)** *around* the chicken, not over it. Season the liquid with **salt, pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil**.
2. Simmer uncovered over medium-low **6–8 minutes**, until the thighs hit **175°F** and the sauce has thickened just enough to lightly coat a spoon — the sharp vinegar smell mellows as it's ready.

### Step 4 — Build the Platter (while it simmers)

1. Arrange the **1 lb sliced tomatoes** and the **reserved raw onion** on a serving platter and **salt them now** — a few minutes' head start gets their juices weeping into what will become the sauce below.

### Step 5 — Assemble

1. Taste the sauce. If it reads lean, swirl in **1 tbsp butter** off heat — it stands in for the fat the bones and extra skin would have given. Adjust salt.
2. Set the chicken on the tomatoes with tongs, then spoon the sauce and onions over everything. Finish with **a drizzle of olive oil, flaky salt, more pepper flakes, and black pepper**. Bread alongside.

## Tips

- **The whole trick** is hot, rich, spicy-sour sauce hitting cold raw tomatoes — an excellent two-for-one chicken and salad. Don't skip salting the tomatoes early.
- **Tomato season rules this dish:** in winter, use the best cherry tomatoes you can find, halved.
- **Leftovers are delicious, just demoted:** the flavor holds beautifully, but the skin loses its crisp. Store chicken and sauce separately from any surviving tomatoes; re-crisp the skin in a dry skillet, or slice the chicken cold over fresh tomatoes tomorrow.
- **The bones you saved** go in a freezer bag toward the next batch of stock — which can then be the ⅓ cup in this very sauce.
- **Heat scales** cleanly through the pepper flakes; 1½ tsp is a medium rumble.
