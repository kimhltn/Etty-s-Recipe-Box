# Slow Cooked Shabbat Lamb (Luxe) — Cook Mode

*The same overnight braise, leveled up: a spice-crust dry brine, anchovy-deepened pot, a blistering high-heat finish, a worked jus, and salsa verde. Start 1–2 days ahead — the extra day is fridge time, not work. House cut: **shoulder**, not leg — more fat and collagen means it can't dry out and renders the fat that crisps the potatoes. As-written sibling: `recipes/slow-cooked-shabbat-lamb.md`.*

**Serves:** 6–10 | **Prep:** ~25 min active (+ 24–48 hr dry brine) | **Cook:** 10–12 hr + 20 min blast + 15 min rest | **Total:** 2 days on the calendar, ~45 min of it active

---

## 🛒 Grocery List

### Meat & Dairy
- [ ] 1 bone-in lamb shoulder, 4–5 lb (*leg works; shoulder is the house cut here*)
- [ ] Greek yogurt or labne (~1½ cups, for serving)

### Produce
- [ ] 2 lb waxy potatoes (Yukon Gold or fingerlings)
- [ ] 2 heads garlic (in whole and unpeeled — plus 1 clove for the yogurt)
- [ ] 3 lemons (1 for the pot, 1 for the salsa verde + yogurt zest, 1 for the table)
- [ ] 1 bunch oregano, marjoram, or thyme
- [ ] 1 big bunch each parsley + mint (need ~1½ cups chopped total, plus dill or chives if you like)
- [ ] 1 small shallot

### Pantry
- [ ] 1 bottle (750 ml) dry white wine — crisp and unfancy, pinot grigio territory
- [ ] 6 anchovy fillets (oil-packed)
- [ ] Capers (need 2 tbsp)
- [ ] Olive oil (~1 cup total across pot, salsa verde, finishing)
- [ ] Kosher salt & black pepper

### Spices
- [ ] Fennel seeds (1 tbsp)
- [ ] Coriander seeds (1 tbsp)

**Equipment check:** a Dutch oven or heavy pot of **at least 6 quarts, with a lid**.

---

## Before You Start (day of — 5 minutes)

*The dry brine happened 1–2 days ago (Step 1). Day-of mise:*

- **Potatoes:** halve any bigger than an egg; leave small ones whole. No peeling.
- **Garlic:** halve both heads crosswise, skins on. Set 1 clove aside for the yogurt.
- **Lemon:** halve 1 lemon crosswise for the pot.
- *Salsa verde and yogurt happen later, while the lamb rests — nothing else to do now.*

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Spice-Crust Dry Brine (24–48 hours ahead — don't skip)

1. Coarsely crack **1 tbsp fennel seeds** and **1 tbsp coriander seeds** (mortar, or a pan bottom on a cutting board). Mix with about **1½ tbsp kosher salt** and **plenty of black pepper**.
2. Rub it all over the **lamb**, into every crevice. Refrigerate **uncovered, 24–48 hours** — the salt penetrates the whole cut and the surface dries out, which pays off at the blast. *Pressed for time? Even overnight beats same-day.*

### Step 2 — Build the Pot (15 minutes, day of)

1. Preheat the oven to **275°F / 135°C**. Set the **lamb** in the Dutch oven and tuck **6 anchovy fillets** around and under it — they dissolve completely into savory depth; nobody will clock them as fish.
2. Scatter the **potatoes**, **2 halved garlic heads**, **halved lemon**, and the **whole herb bunch** around the lamb.
3. Pour the **entire bottle of wine** over, then a **long pour of olive oil** — a visible, thick layer floating on top.

### Step 3 — The Long Nothing (10–12 hours)

1. Lid on. Into the oven for **10–12 hours** — do not look, peek, or touch. Done when the meat **shreds at the touch of a spoon and the bone wiggles free**; an extra hour won't hurt it.

### Step 4 — The Blast (20 minutes — the big upgrade)

1. Pull the pot out, lid off, and crank the oven to **475°F**. While it heats *(~10 minutes — start Step 5 now)*, spoon a little pot fat over any potatoes sitting above the liquid.
2. Return the pot, uncovered, to the **top third** of the oven for **15–20 minutes**, until the lamb's cap is **deeply browned and blistered** and the exposed potatoes have caught color in the lamb fat. Check at 12 minutes — it moves fast at the end. *Broiler works too; watch it like a hawk.*

### Step 5 — Salsa Verde & Yogurt (while the oven heats / lamb blasts)

1. Finely chop **~1½ cups parsley and mint** and mix with **2 tbsp capers** (roughly chopped), **1 small shallot, minced**, the **zest and juice of ½ lemon**, **⅓ cup olive oil**, and a pinch of salt. It should be spoonable and sharp.
2. Stir **~1½ cups yogurt or labne** with **1 grated garlic clove**, the **zest of the other ½ lemon**, salt, and pepper. Refrigerate both until serving.

### Step 6 — Rest & Work the Jus (15 minutes)

1. Move the lamb and potatoes to a platter; tent loosely with foil and rest **15 minutes**.
2. Tilt the pot and spoon off most of the clear fat on top. **Save 2 tbsp** — whisk **1 tbsp, warm, into the salsa verde** (trust this) and bank the rest for roast potatoes another day.
3. Fish out the collapsed **lemon halves**, squeeze their soft confit pulp and juice back into the pot, and discard the rinds. Squeeze the cloves from **one garlic head** out of their skins into the juices, mash them in with a fork, and whisk. Taste — it likely wants a pinch of salt, maybe more lemon. That's your jus. *(The second garlic head goes to the table for bread.)*

### Step 7 — Serve

1. Spoon **jus** generously over the lamb and potatoes, top with **salsa verde** (rest in a bowl at the table), and serve with the **lemony garlic yogurt** and **lemon wedges**.

## Tips

- **Overnight math:** brine Thursday night, pot in the oven Friday 9 pm, blast + rest Saturday morning, reheat for Saturday dinner. It reheats beautifully — covered, **275°F** — and the blast can even happen *after* reheating if you're cooking fully ahead.
- **Make-ahead:** keeps covered in the fridge **3–4 days**. Salsa verde is best within a day; yogurt keeps 3.
- **The saved lamb fat** is a prize — roast potatoes, fried eggs, crisping leftover lamb.
- **Leftovers:** shred into pasta with a ladle of jus, or flatbreads with the yogurt and salsa verde.
- **Dial it back:** every upgrade here detaches cleanly — skip the blast and it's still better than as-written from the brine + anchovies + jus alone.
